A TEA RITUAL

RE-DESIGNING AN EVERYDAY

TEA RITUAL

by Gabriele Barzilai,  Thijs Roeleven,  Roy Gevers, Xihao Hu, Yijun Yu, Huang Zang

In this project we designed a tea ritual, starting from the analysis of the group members' needs and values related to the everyday tea ritual. The ritual includes physical objects, symbolic gestures, specific sequences in the preparation and sharing of tea. In the design process much attention was given to the semantic difference between routine and ritual, focusing on the latter. In this view, what makes a daily practice a ritual rather than a routine is the attribution of values to the specific practice by the individual who is performing it.

Every single aspect of the whole experience of the tea ritual, from aesthetics to materials and gestures was carefully designed following the synthesis of the values come out from the group discussions. Given the international character of the design team, the final design inevitably assumed a flavour of mixed cultural values and tastes. The tea set mostly expresses such a multicultural character, combining western and eastern features together.
